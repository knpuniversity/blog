# Meet Brontie, your AI Companion

We've been quietly working on something fun, and today's the day: meet **Brontie** —
your new SymfonyCasts companion. Part dinosaur, part AI, fully obsessed with Symfony.

You'll find Brontie hanging out in the comments of every tutorial — ready to help
when you get stuck on a tricky chapter, can't remember which
service to autowire, or just want a second opinion on your code.

# How to Summon Brontie

Brontie lives in comments. To get Brontie's attention, mention `@Brontie` in any
comment on a chapter, course, or blog post.

That's it! Brontie will pop in with an answer shortly after. Want to keep the
conversation going? Just reply like you would to anyone else — Brontie
remembers the thread.

# Brontie has Watched Every Tutorial on SymfonyCasts

Here's the part we're really proud of: Brontie is trained on every SymfonyCasts
tutorial. When you ask a question, Brontie pulls from the actual lessons we've
written.

Brontie has also been studying the official Symfony documentation and source
code, Doctrine, EasyAdmin, PHPUnit docs, and all the other libraries we cover
throughout our courses — so when Brontie points you to a method or feature,
it's grounded in the real ecosystem you're working in.

That means Brontie's answers stay closer to the version of Symfony you're
learning — and far less likely to reach for a method that was removed several versions ago,
or an invented API. Brontie is an AI and won't always be perfect, but the tutorials
and docs help keep Brontie honest.

# Help Brontie get Smarter

Every answer Brontie gives has a rating widget. If Brontie nailed it, say so.
If Brontie missed the mark, tell us what went wrong — we read the feedback and
use it to sharpen Brontie up over time.

# Joining the Beta

Brontie is currently in closed beta for SymfonyCasts subscribers. We're letting
people in gradually so we can keep an eye on quality and make sure Brontie is actually helping.

If you're a subscriber, head over to the [Brontie page](https://symfonycasts.com/brontie/beta) and request access.

Happy chatting! 🦕
